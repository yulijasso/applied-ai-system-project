# Feeding Schedules for Dogs and Cats

**Dogs**:
- Adult (1–7 years for most breeds): 2 meals/day is the standard veterinary recommendation, typically 8–12 hours apart. Once-daily feeding is associated with higher GDV risk in deep-chested breeds and is not recommended for them.
- Puppy: 3–4 meals/day until ~6 months, then 2 meals/day for most breeds. Large/giant breeds often stay on 3 meals/day until 6 months and then 2 meals/day, with growth-formula food until ~12–24 months depending on breed size.
- Senior: 2 meals/day for most; condition-specific splits (e.g., 3 smaller meals) help dogs with reduced appetite, nausea on empty stomach, or chronic GI disease.
- Free-feeding (food left out) is not recommended for dogs — it complicates portion control, house-training, and detection of appetite changes that are often the first sign of illness.

**Cats**:
- Adult: 2–3 measured meals/day is the most commonly recommended pattern (Cornell Feline Health Center; VCA). Cats fed 8–10+ hours after a meal show physiological hunger signaling — gaps over ~12 hours between meals are not ideal.
- Kitten: 3–4 meals/day until ~6 months; transition to adult schedule by ~12 months.
- Senior/geriatric: 2–3 meals/day; cats with CKD or hyperthyroidism often do better on 4 small meals plus all-day water access.
- Free-feeding dry food is associated with overweight/obesity in indoor cats; if used, the daily ration should still be measured rather than topped up ad lib.

**Diabetic dogs and cats (insulin-dependent)**:
- Insulin must be paired with a meal. Typical dosing is twice daily, ~12 hours apart, given with or immediately after the meal.
- If the pet refuses the meal, the standard owner instruction is to give a partial dose or skip and contact the veterinary team — giving full insulin to a fasted diabetic can produce life-threatening hypoglycemia.
- Meal timing and composition should not vary day to day; insulin doses are titrated to a specific feeding pattern.

**Inter-meal spacing**:
- For an adult dog or cat on a standard 2-meals/day plan, intervals of 8–12 hours between meals are typical. A second feeding under ~4 hours after the first is unusual outside of medical 4-meal-a-day plans (e.g., feline CKD, post-op recovery, very small toy-breed puppies).

**Water**:
- Fresh water available at all times for both species. Bowls cleaned daily — biofilm forms within 24 hours.
- Many cats prefer running or wide, shallow bowls and drink more from fountains; this matters most in cats prone to lower urinary tract disease and CKD.

(akc.org); International Cat Care, "Feeding your cat or kitten" (icatcare.org).
