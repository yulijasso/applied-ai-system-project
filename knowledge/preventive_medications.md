# Preventive Medications: Heartworm, Flea, and Tick

The American Heartworm Society and the Companion Animal Parasite Council both recommend year-round administration of heartworm and flea/tick preventives across all of North America. The "think 12" framing from AHS captures the standard: 12 months of prevention each year, plus a heartworm test every 12 months.

**Heartworm prevention (dogs)**:
- Monthly oral preventives are the most common option: ivermectin-based (Heartgard, Tri-Heart Plus, Iverhart), milbemycin oxime (Interceptor, Sentinel), moxidectin (Advantage Multi topical, ProHeart injectable).
- Injectable moxidectin (ProHeart 6 / ProHeart 12) is administered every 6 or 12 months by a veterinarian.
- Dose timing for monthly oral preventives: same calendar day each month. Monthly products provide a "reachback" effect against larvae acquired in the prior ~30 days; intervals significantly longer than ~6 weeks open a window in which infection can establish.
- Ivermectin and milbemycin chews are best given with a small meal — fat improves absorption of these lipophilic drugs.
- Pre-prescription and annual testing: dogs should be tested for heartworm antigen (and ideally microfilariae) before starting a preventive and at least every 12 months thereafter, even on continuous prevention. Giving a fast-killing macrocyclic lactone to a dog with a heavy circulating microfilarial load can produce shock-like reactions.

**Heartworm prevention (cats)**:
- Indoor-only status does not eliminate risk — mosquitoes enter homes; cats acquire infection at lower rates than dogs but tolerate it far worse, with sudden death a frequent presentation.
- Approved feline products include topical Advantage Multi (moxidectin/imidacloprid), topical Revolution Plus (selamectin/sarolaner), and oral Heartgard for Cats (ivermectin).

**Flea and tick prevention**:
- Most oral isoxazoline products in dogs (NexGard, Simparica, Credelio) are labeled monthly. Bravecto chewables in dogs are labeled every 12 weeks for fleas and most ticks (every 8 weeks for lone-star ticks). Bravecto Quantum injectable (dogs) is labeled every 12 months, with shorter intervals where lone-star tick coverage is required.
- Topical and collar products vary: Frontline Plus monthly; Seresto collar up to 8 months; Revolution monthly.
- Year-round dosing is recommended in nearly all U.S. regions. Adult fleas survive indoors year-round, and ticks (notably Ixodes) are active any day above ~40°F / 4°C, including during winter warm spells.
- In cats, isoxazoline options are more limited but include Bravecto for cats (topical, every 8–12 weeks depending on parasite) and Revolution Plus.

**Combination products** simplify owner adherence:
- Simparica Trio (sarolaner + moxidectin + pyrantel) — monthly, dogs.
- NexGard Plus (afoxolaner + moxidectin + pyrantel) — monthly, dogs.
- Trifexis (spinosad + milbemycin) — monthly, dogs (no tick coverage).
- Revolution Plus — monthly, cats (no tapeworm coverage).

**Common scheduling errors**:
- A daily recurrence configured for a monthly preventive is an error; the correct cadence is monthly (or every 12 weeks for Bravecto chewables, etc.).
- Dosing a heartworm chew on an empty stomach reduces absorption.
- Stopping prevention "for the winter" in temperate U.S. climates is no longer supported by AHS or CAPC.
- Stacking multiple preventives on different days of the month worsens adherence; one consolidated "preventive day" each month is the standard recommendation.

Source: American Heartworm Society, "Preventives" and current Canine/Feline Heartworm Guidelines (heartwormsociety.org); Companion Animal Parasite Council, "The Case for Year-Round Flea and Tick Control" and species-specific guidelines (capcvet.org); AVMA, "AHS updates heartworm guidelines for dogs" (avma.org); VCA Animal Hospitals, "Flea and Tick Prevention" and "Fluralaner" monographs (vcahospitals.com).
